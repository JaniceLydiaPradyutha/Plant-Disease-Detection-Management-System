Farmer Tips Exchange

Share your farming wisdom and learn from experienced growers. Practical tips for better yields and sustainable practices.

@SoilExpert

Agricultural Scientist

Always test your soil pH before planting season. A simple $15 test kit can save you hundreds in failed crops and unnecessary fertilizers. Most vegetables prefer a pH between 6.0 and 7.0. If your soil is too acidic, add lime; if it's too alkaline, add sulfur.

@WaterWise

Irrigation Specialist

Water your plants early in the morning to reduce evaporation loss. Evening watering can promote fungal diseases due to overnight moisture. For most crops, deep, infrequent watering is better than frequent shallow watering as it encourages deeper root growth.
